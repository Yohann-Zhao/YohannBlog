Part 2 & 3: Winds and Stability

Items marked with an asterisk (*) are not required for exam preparation but are recommended to review for broader context or supplementary knowledge.

Coriolis Effect

Southern Hemisphere: Causes anti-clockwise rotation around low-pressure systems.

Pressure Gradient

Definition: Rate of pressure change over distance.

Impact:

Determines wind speed (closer isobars = steeper gradient = stronger winds).

Isobar spacing indicates gradient strength.

Pressure Belt Systems

Key belts: Equatorial Trough, Monsoonal Trough.

Individual Pressure Cells

High-Pressure System:

Air subsides (sinks), flows outward at the surface.

Southern Hemisphere: Deflected left (anti-clockwise).

Low-Pressure System:

Pressure gradient force > Coriolis.

Air spirals upward (clockwise in S. Hemisphere).

Synoptic Charts

Stations: Data corrected to sea-level pressure.

Isobars:

Lines of equal pressure.

Close spacing = steep gradient = strong winds.

Isotherms: Lines of equal temperature.

Cyclonic Flow (S. Hemisphere)

Low-Pressure (Cyclone): Clockwise rotation.

High-Pressure: Anti-clockwise rotation.

Global Wind Cells

1.    Tropical Cell: SE trades (SW monsoon).

2.    Sub-Tropical Ridge: High-pressure zone.

3.    Mid-Latitude Cell: Westerlies.

4.    Sub-Polar Low: Polar front.

5.    Polar Cell: Polar high.

 

Condition

Impact

SE trades blow east-west; warm water in W. Pacific; rains in Australia.

El Niño

Warm water shifts east; weak/reversed trades; drought in Australia.

La Niña

Strengthened SE trades; above-average rainfall in Australia.

Buys Ballot’s Law (S. Hemisphere)

Stand with back to wind; low pressure on your right.

Wind Speed & Behavior

Surface Wind:

Slowest at midnight-dawn (minimal cross-isobar flow).

Speed increases with altitude (friction decreases).

Above 3,000ft:

Gradient flow: Parallel to isobars.

Geostrophic flow: Straight isobars (Coriolis = pressure gradient).

Wind Direction Changes

Veering: Clockwise shift (e.g., descending air).

Backing: Anti-clockwise shift (e.g., climbing air).

Surface Wind Behavior

Veering: Clockwise shift (e.g., descending air).

Backing: Anti-clockwise shift (e.g., climbing air).

Veering/Backing with Altitude

Over Land:

Wind veers (clockwise shift) by ~30° with increasing altitude.

Over Sea:

Wind veers by ~10° with increasing altitude.

Speed Reduction

Surface Wind Speed:

Lowest at midnight-dawn (minimal cross-isobar flow).

Over Land: Drops by ~2/3 at the surface.

Over Sea: Drops by ~1/3 at the surface.

Wind Shear

Vertical Shear:

Common in morning inversions (cold surface, warm air aloft).

Turbulence near inversion layers.

Horizontal Shear: Near thunderstorms (updrafts/downdrafts).

Local Winds

Sea Breeze:

Mid-afternoon (strongest temperature contrast).

Flows from sea to land (local low over land).

Land Breeze: Weak, pre-dawn flow (land to sea).

Katabatic: Cold air drainage (sloping terrain).

Anabatic: Daytime upslope winds.

Diurnal Variation

Wind: Stronger/backed by day; weaker/veered at night.

Factors:

Vertical mixing (reduces at night).

Cloud cover/humidity impact temperature.

Low-Level Jet Streams

Nighttime speed increase (40-50 kt at 2,000 ft).

Causes severe wind shear (turbulence near terrain).

Humidity & Stability

Dew Point: Temperature for 100% RH.

Cloud Formation: Requires condensation nuclei + latent heat.

Stability:

Stable Air: Parcel stops rising.

Unstable Air: Parcel continues rising (ELR > DALR).

Adiabatic Processes

DALR: 3°C/1,000 ft (dry air).

SALR: 1.5°C/1,000 ft (saturated air).

Föhn Wind: Warm, dry lee-side wind (latent heat release on windward).

Temperature Inversions

Types:

1.    Radiation (surface cooling).

2.    Frontal (warm air over cold).

3.    Subsidence (high-pressure sinking).

Flight Impact: Turbulence at inversion boundaries.
