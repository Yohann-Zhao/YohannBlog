Key Requirements

Condensation nuclei (e.g., dust, salt particles).

Water vapor (must reach saturation).

Dew point temperature (cooling to this temperature initiates condensation).

Droplet Size & Color

Max droplet size depends on:

    • Mass vs. Drag (mass ↑, drag ↑).
    • Updraft strength (stronger updrafts = larger droplets).

Color: Larger droplets = darker cloud shades.

Cloud Formation Process

1.    Cooling to dew point:

o    Most common via adiabatic expansion (rising air cools).

2.    Rising mechanisms:

o    Convection (ELR-driven):

§  Latitudinal heating (e.g., polar air moving north behind cold fronts in Australia).

§  Creates Cumulus (Cu) or thunderstorms (TS).

§  Occurs day/night, driven by temperature gradients.

o    Orographic uplift (air forced over terrain).

o    Frontal uplift (air rises along weather fronts).

o    Convergence:

§  Widespread uplift → Stratus (St)-like clouds.

o    Turbulence:

§  Forms Stratocumulus (Sc) at turbulent layer tops.

§  Dissipates at sunset.

Australia-Specific Formation

Polar air moving north behind cold fronts:

    • Surface conduction cooling + upper cold air → strong Cumulus (Cu).
