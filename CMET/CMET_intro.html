2. 潜热（Latent Heat）

2.1 Latent Heat Absorption

Latent heat is absorbed when a substance undergoes a phase change that requires energy input. This typically occurs during processes where the substance moves to a higher energy state.

Process

Description

Example in Meteorology

Evaporation

Liquid → Gas (requires energy to break molecular bonds)

Water evaporating from the ocean surface, absorbing heat from the surrounding environment.

Sublimation

Solid → Gas (requires energy to transition directly from solid to gas)

Dry ice (solid CO₂) sublimating into gas at room temperature, absorbing heat.

Melting

Solid → Liquid (requires energy to break the solid structure)

Ice melting into liquid water, absorbing heat from the environment (e.g., snowmelt).

2.2 Latent Heat Release

Latent heat is released when a substance undergoes a phase change that releases energy. This typically occurs during processes where the substance moves to a lower energy state.

Process

Description

Example in Meteorology

Condensation

Gas → Liquid (releases energy as molecules form bonds)

Water vapor condensing into droplets in clouds, releasing heat into the atmosphere.

Deposition

Gas → Solid (releases energy as molecules form a solid structure)

Frost forming on surfaces when water vapor deposits directly as ice crystals.

Freezing

Liquid → Solid (releases energy as molecules form a solid structure)

Liquid water freezing into ice, releasing heat into the surrounding environment.

2.3 Meteorological Significance

  • Weather Systems: Latent heat release during condensation is a primary energy source for thunderstorms and hurricanes.
  • Temperature Regulation: Latent heat exchange plays a critical role in regulating Earth's temperature and climate.
  • Cloud Formation: Latent heat release during condensation helps sustain cloud formation and precipitation processes.

2.4 Exam Tips

[!IMPORTANT] Exam Focus:

- Identify whether latent heat is absorbed or released in a given phase change.

 

[!STRATEGY] Memory Aid:

- **Absorption**: Think of processes that require energy (e.g., melting ice, evaporating water).

- **Release**: Think of processes that produce energy (e.g., forming dew, freezing water).

    • 定义: QNH 是指将机场标高处的气压调整到平均海平面（Mean Sea Level, MSL）的气压值。
    • 用途: 用于将高度表设置为显示海拔高度（Altitude above MSL）。

QFE:
    • 定义: QFE 是指机场标高处的实际气压值。
    • 用途: 用于将高度表设置为显示相对于机场的高度（Height above Aerodrome）。
    • 特点: 当高度表设置为QFE时，飞机在机场跑道上时高度表显示为0。
  • ISA (International Standard Atmosphere):
    • 定义: ISA 是国际标准大气模型，用于描述大气压力、温度和密度随高度的变化。
    • 标准条件:
      • 海平面气压：1013.25 hPa
      • 海平面温度：15°C
      • 温度递减率：2°C/1000 ft
    • 用途: 作为参考标准，用于计算密度高度和飞机性能。
