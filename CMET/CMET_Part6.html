Type

Operational Impact

Radiation Fog

Cold surface conduction (night)

Clear skies, light wind, humidity

VIS <1,000m; thickens at sunrise

Advection Fog

Warm humid air over cold surface

Persistent, hard to forecast

Common in W. Australia, Tasman Sea

Slant VIS: Not visible from approach angle (critical for landing).

Mountain Waves (Standing Waves)

Conditions:

    • Wind perpendicular to mountain, speed >25 kt at peak.
    • Stable atmosphere (common in winter).

Effects:

    • Severe turbulence, rotor clouds, downdrafts.

Pilot Action:

    • Maintain ≥1,000 ft clearance; approach obliquely.

Clouds:

    • Lenticular (wave crests), roll clouds (rotors).

Airframe Icing

Types

Type

Formation

Characteristics

Rime

Small supercooled droplets (-10°C to -30°C)

Brittle, white, on leading edges

Clear

Large droplets (>0°C to -15°C)

Heavy, smooth, hard to remove

Critical Areas: Thin structures (radio aerials, wingtips) ice first.

Cloud Icing:

    • Rime: Thin As/Ac, top of Cu.
    • Clear: Thick Cu/Cb/Ns near freezing level.

Freezing Rain

Risk: Rapid clear ice buildup below cloud base.

Action: Descend below freezing level; use anti-ice systems.

Frost & Hoar Frost

Frost:

    • Frozen dew → reduces lift, increases drag.
    • Remove pre-flight (water wash).

Hoar Frost:

    • Forms on cold-soaked surfaces → momentary visibility loss.

Thunderstorms (TS)

Development Stages

1.    Developing: Updrafts dominate.

2.    Mature: Updrafts + downdrafts → severe turbulence, hail, lightning.

3.    Dissipating: Downdrafts weaken.

Hazards

Hazard

Risk

Turbulence

Severe buffeting near cell

Hail

10,000–20,000 ft altitude

Lightning

Electrical system damage

Microbursts

Low-Level Threats

Dust Storms: Reduced VIS (dry convection).

Tornadoes: Extreme winds (funnel clouds).

Waterspouts: Tornadoes over water.

Tropical Cyclones

Conditions:

    • 5°–15° latitude, warm ocean (>26°C).

Lifecycle:

1.    Formative: Convergence → latent heat release.

2.    Mature: Eye forms; destructive winds (front-left quadrant).

3.    Dissipating: Landfall → torrential rain/floods.
