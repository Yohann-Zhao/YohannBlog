Air Masses

Polar Cell: Polar air (cold, dry).

Tropical Air: Moisture-rich.

    • Continental: Over land (dry).
    • Maritime: Over sea (humid).

Fronts

Definition: Boundary between air masses of different densities (little mixing).

Polar Front:

    • Separates polar cell from mid-latitude/subtropical cells.
    • Wave-like disturbances:
      • Stable waves die out; unstable waves form eddies (cold/warm fronts).

Cold Front (Australia Winter)

Movement: Sweeps across southern Australia; replaces tropical air with polar air.

Conditions:

    • Ahead of front:
      • Squall line, severe turbulence, TCU/Cb/TS (if ELR < DALR).
    • After passage:
      • Mid-clouds, limited Cu due to frontal inversion.

Wind Shift:

    • Before: NW wind.
    • After: S/W wind (backed).

Warm Front

Formation: South of Australia; moves southward (minimal impact).

Conditions:

    • Approach: Ci/Cs → Ac/As → Ns (continuous rain).
    • After: Rapid clearing (stable warm air over cold ground).

Occluded Front

Cold front overtakes warm front → strong lifting at occlusion.

Pressure Systems

High-Pressure (Anticyclone)

Features:

    • Isobars close around high.
    • Weather: Sinking air → clear skies, occasional Cu/Sc.

Ridge: Elongated high → stable weather (hazardous near tropical maritime air).

Low-Pressure (Depression)

Features:

    • Isobars close around low → convergence → Ns/Cb/heavy rain.
    • Tropical Cyclone: Intense low over warm ocean.

Trough: Dashed line (strong convergence → severe weather).

Col

Region between two highs/lows → light, variable winds.

Key Rules & Formulas

Pressure Gradient:

    • High to Low, Look out below (QNH drops → lower true altitude).
    • Low to High, Watch the sky (rising air → cloud/precipitation).

Pressure Height (P.H.):

P.H. = Indicated Alt + (1013 – QNH) × 30 ft/hPa

Synoptic Chart Analysis

Cold Fronts: Never originate from high centers.

Isobars:

    • Spacing (~4 hPa) indicates gradient strength.
    • Direction consistent except at col (reverses).

Regional Climate Features (Australia)

Winter

Southern Australia:

    • Cold fronts dominate (strong westerlies "Roaring Forties").

Northern Australia:

    • High-pressure → dry, stable weather.

Summer

Southern Australia:

    • Variable weather; troughs/monsoonal influence (north).

Northern Australia:

    • Monsoonal trough → heavy rain, thunderstorms.

Latitudinal Notes

Tropic of Capricorn: ~23°S (divides tropical/subtropical zones).

40°S: Strong westerlies ("Brave Westerlies").
