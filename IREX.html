How to use this note Edited 2025.11.16

IREX exam is about 2 things:

  • IFR Procedures & Charts — practical; learned from the handbook.
  • IFR Law — theoretical; requirements and regulatory references.

This note is intended to help you learn the IFR law part by collecting relevant references (AIP, MOS, ENR). Read and highlight the referenced sections, then practice exam questions.

Remember additional theoretical items: radio‑nav errors (VOR, NDB), GNSS topics (RAIM, AIAC), and general regulatory requirements.

IFR ACFT

Flight Instrument

References: 91 MOS Ch26; 135 MOS Ch11.07

Summary:

  • VFR: IAS, P.H., magnetic heading, time.
  • AWK: turn & slip, outside air temperature.
  • NVFR: altimeter, VSI, stable heading; monitor power source to gyros.
  • IFR: alternate static source, pitot heat required where applicable.
  • 135 MOS: dual altimeter & attitude indicator for certain ops.

Navigation Instrument

References: 91 MOS Ch14 14.02; ENR 1.1 4.5; 135 MOS Ch11 11.09

Summary:

  • Area navigation (GNSS) permitted.
  • Ground aid tracking: ±9° of next aid rated range; position fix validity up to 2 hours as applicable.
  • Day VMC VFR position fix requirement: 30 minutes.
  • NVFR: minimum 1 navigation aid.
  • IFR (135): 2 approved GNSS (not C129) or 1 GNSS + ADF/VOR.
  • Failure of any aid: aircraft should be able to continue according to the flight plan and meet ATC/airspace requirements.

Radio Communication

Communication requirements include transponder and VHF. See ERSA EMRG for lost‑comms procedures.

Lost radio under IMC: squawk 7600, follow latest/expected clearance per flight plan. Mnemonic guidance: 1 hold, 2 vector, 3 limit (hold 1, if vectored wait 2 min, at limit hold 3 min then continue as expected). Use other means (beacon, phone) where possible.

Automatic Pilot

Reference: 135 MOS Ch11 11.10

Single‑pilot operations allowed NVFR/IFR. Required modes: altitude and heading. If autopilot inoperative, day VMC may be acceptable depending on operation.

Alerting & Warning System

Reference: 91 MOS Ch26 26.25

Altimeter serviceability checks

Reference: 91 MOS Ch10 10.4

  • IFR tolerance: ±60 ft.
  • Inoperative altimeter indicated by >±75 ft.
  • If discrepancy 60–75 ft, check again at first AD; if still outside tolerance treat as inoperative.

IFR Flight Plan & ENR

Pre Flight consideration

Plan in accordance with ENR and MOS requirements; check LSALT, minimum heights and other ENR references.

Flight Notification

Reference: ENR 1.10 — IFR flights must submit flight notification/flight plan.

Forecast

Reference: ENR 1.10

Forecast coverage: 30 min before ETA to 60 min after ETA for DEP/ARR/ALTN checks. See GEN and ENR LSALT guidance and 91.305 minimum heights.

Positive Radio Fix

Reference: ENR 1.1 4.5

Alternate & Hold

Mnemonic for alternate factors: "Alternate Could Very Well Proved Life Savers + Hold"

(Aids, Cloud, Visibility, Wind, PROB, Lights, Storm) + Holding considerations.

Aids

Reference: 91 MOS Ch8 (8.08, 8.05); 135 MOS Ch11 11.09

Day IFRFly to AD without available IAP or where IAP cannot be used; use GAF LSALT for final route +500 ft; 8 km visibility rule for visual approach.
Night IFRFly to AD without available IAP or where IAP cannot be used (night IFP must have IAP where required).
Night VFRIf ground aids/GNSS cannot be used enroute, nominate an alternate within 1 hour.

C129 receivers cannot be used for alternate navigation qualification.

135 MOS: Night VFR must have GNSS or ADF/VOR; barometric aiding must be connected if provided. IFR requires two approved GNSS (not C129) or one GNSS + ADF/VOR. Failure of any aid should still allow navigation to meet flight plan and ATC/airspace requirements; possible to land at IMC AD by navigating to a point enabling visual approach/IAP landing.

Wind 

Buffer rules: 30 minute buffer generally applies. TAF3 first 3 hours: 30 minute buffer and PROB do not apply; TEMPO cessation rules may reduce required time (e.g., 10 min).

IFR arrival planning: check the approach plate, cloud and visibility per ENR 1.5 6.1.1. If approach plate not usable, day procedures use LSALT + 500 ft and 8 km visibility per ENR 1.1 10.7.2.10.

Performance category and MDH notes: If shaded on IAP, use Actual QNH -100 ft; area forecast QNH add +50 ft. See ENR 1.5.5.3 for QNH sources and ENR 1.5 6.2.1 and 91 MOS Ch8 for special alternate minima.

Wind

Use aircraft‑specific maximum crosswind component limits for planning and landing decisions.

Holding

Reference: ENR 1.1 10.7.2

  • Intermediate (INTER) requirement: 30 min
  • Temporary (TEMP) requirement: 60 min
  • Not cumulative
  • No need after 30 min cessation of the specified period
  • FM / BECMG changes: apply 30 min buffer

TAF3 — first 3 hours

TAF3 first 3 hours: 30 minute buffer does not apply; PROB groups do not apply.

Fuel

References: 91 MOS Ch19 19.02; 135 MOS Ch7 7.02

IFR final reserve fuel (general rule): 45 minutes (piston example used in exams).

Airspace

References: 91 MOS 21.05; ENR 1.1 2.9; ENR 1.1 2.8; ENR 1.4–1.4.2

Two basic types: CTA and OCTA. Controlled airspace includes CTA and CTR. Classes overview:

  • Class D: radar not available; procedural methods used. IFR separated from IFR & SVFR; VFR receives traffic information.
  • Class C: radar available. IFR separated from IFR, SVFR, VFR; VFR receives separation from IFR and traffic information about other VFR.

Provide standard departure report: time, track, height. See ENR references for detailed airspace structure and ATC requirements.

Select Route

Use ERSA GEN‑FPR and Flight Planning Options for route selection. Check recommended routes, departures, ERC & TAC and applicable airspace along the route.

IFR IAP

Most important practical section — review IFR handbook and IAP charts thoroughly. Practice interpreting approach plates and circling minima.

Key references: ENR 1.5 1.14 (Visual Approach); ENR 1.5 para 1.6.6 (Circling Area); ENR 1.5 1.2.1; ENR 1.5 2.4.4; ENR 1.5 2.8; ENR 1.5 4.4; ENR 1.6.6; ENR 1.7.
