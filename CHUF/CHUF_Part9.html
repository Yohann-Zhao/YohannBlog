Part 9: Error, Behaviour, Stress, Fatigue

Aides-Mémoire/Memory Methods

1.    Turn unrelated things into mnemonic

2.    written checklist (better method)

Improving recall quality:

Pleasant experiences enhance memory.

Logical connections improve retention.

The Mindset

Pattern Recognition: The brain seeks familiar patterns in long-term memory → tendency to assume similarity.

How to avoid - Judgment Training:

Consider all inputs and all facts before making a decision.

Avoid rushing to conclusions or focusing on limited information.

Problem Solving (PILOT Model)

1.    P – Problem Facts: Gather all relevant information.

2.    I - Define/Identify: define the core issue.

3.    L - Look for Solution: Explore possible solutions.

4.    O - Act/Operate: Implement the chosen solution.

5.    T - Test Result: Evaluate the outcome.

Review and Repeat Process → If not, risk of Confirmation Bias: Ignoring evidence that contradicts the initial assumption.

False Hypothesis Formation

1.   High expectancy bias: Before flight find left engine not good. In flight when one engine failed, you immediately turn off left engine, only to find it’s the engine on the right has failed.

2.   Diverted attention: Busy with ATC before landing, forget about landing gear down.

3.   Psychological defense mechanism (desiring a positive outcome): When looking out for weather, pilot assume(wish) the weather is getting better.

4.   Following intense concentration: After a demanding lesson’s landing, you finally relaxed, then forget what you should do after landing.

5.   Effects of motor memory: Following motor memory about flying faster, you retract landing gear and flap on final approach to make more traffic separation, then forget to landing gear down again on touchdown.

6.   Environmental capture (habitual response to a familiar environment): Whenever on final approach, he/she assumed before landing checklist has been done. (which has normally been done by this stage)

7.   Disorientation at night:

Initially navigating visually.

Then Entering clouds → failure to monitor Directional Gyro (DG) → deviation.

Exiting clouds → mistaking the wrong end of the runway for the intended approach. Land on the opposite side.

Group Decision-Making & Risk

Risky Shift (Group Polarization): Groups may make riskier decisions or more conservative ones than individuals.

Stress

Physiological Response: Adrenaline release

Types:

1.    Acute (short-term, immediate response)

2.    Chronic (long-term, cumulative stress)

Indicators of Stress:

Mental: Confusion, poor concentration

Physical: Fatigue, headaches

Emotional: Anxiety, irritability

Behavioral: Poor decision-making, reckless actions

Stressors:

Negative events (e.g., emergencies, workload)

Positive events (e.g., promotion, new responsibilities)

Optimal Arousal:

Moderate levels enhance performance; extremes impair it.

Stages of Stress Response:

1.   Alarm (initial reaction)

2.   Resistance (attempt to escape)

3.   Collapse (exhaustion due to prolonged stress)

Coping Strategies:

Action-Coping: Avoidance, escapism

Cognitive-Coping: Rationalization, seeking perspective, counseling

Symptom-Directed: Seeking medical help, exercise, medication

Managing Stress:

Acknowledge stress → Avoid denial/internalization

Identify stressors and modify behavior, attitude, fitness

Adopt stress-reducing habits (relaxation, time management, preparation)

Fatigue

Types:

Acute: Temporary, recoverable with rest.

Chronic: Long-term, lifestyle or job-related; linked to chronic stress → requires lifestyle change.

Effects:

Monotonous tasks deteriorate first.

Complex tasks require more effort, leading to faster fatigue.

Body Rhythms & Sleep

General Sleep Patterns:

Optimal sleep time: 10 PM – 6 AM

Circadian Rhythms:

Morning: Low body temperature & performance.

Post-lunch dip: Natural drop in alertness.

Afternoon: Higher temperature & performance.

Trend: Tendency to shift later (1–1.5 hours/day) if unrestricted.

Sleep/Wake Cycle:

Credit System:

Maximum: 16 points

+2 points per hour of sleep

-1 point per hour awake

Readiness to sleep: Influenced by circadian rhythm.

Ideal sleep duration: 8–9 hours.

Sleep Stages:

Alpha (Resting)

Beta (Normal wakefulness)

90-Minute Sleep Cycle:

Stage 1

Stage 2 (Majority of sleep)

Stage 3

Stage 4 (REM, paradoxical sleep)

Sleep Disorders

Narcolepsy (uncontrollable sleep episodes)

Apnea (breathing interruptions during sleep)

Somnambulism (sleepwalking)

Insomnia (hard to fall asleep):

Clinical (long-term)

Situational (short-term, stress-induced)

Jet Lag (Transmeridian Dyschronism)

Flying East: Body rhythms must advance (harder to adjust).

Flying West: Body rhythms must retard (easier to adjust).
