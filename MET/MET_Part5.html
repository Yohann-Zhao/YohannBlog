polar cell: polar air

others: tropical air

(moisture)

over land: continental

over sea: maritime

 

fronts:

different densities, little mixing

boundary:front

 

polar front:

demarcation between polar cell, sub-tropical cell(mid,upper level)

wave like disturbances:

  some stable die away

  some unstable: eddies, seperate, sweep across AUS

    wave front moves N.,polar air replaces tropical air, cold front     wave front moves S.,polar air retreats, warm front

    eddies own frontal system

  Warm front: form to S. AUS, moves S.;little significance

  Cold front:

     prominent feature of AUS winter.

     Sweep across S. half continent. Dissipate, re-form throughly

 

 

Cold front normally before a High

->cold front sweeping S. AUS; warm front form S. to AUS,pushed to SE ->High pressure circulate clockewise about a low S. to AUS

 

 

Wave depression:

  low, between cold, warm waves,

  S. of AUS continent

Occluded front:

  cold, heavier, surge forward faster

  cool catches up warm front, over runs

  warm air trapped, strong lifting at occlusion

 

 

 

 

Cold Front Conditions:

  (clouds formation depends on humidity,stability,speed)

  just ahead of front: Squall line

     gusts, squalls;

     thickening Mid clouds;

     fast low cloud, severe turbulence;

     (if ELR<DALR) a line of TCU, Cb, TS

   further back along slope:

     more Mid clouds;

     frontal inversion:

         Cu limited vertical development

   days after passage:

     allow TCU, small Cb

 

 

Upper westerly winds:

push surface system further east

  Before Cold front: warm NW wind

  Cold front arrives: cold S wind

      backed direction

  After Cold front:  another forming

 

 

Warm Front conditions:

  First noticed:  (stable warm)

    High clouds, Ci,Cs, helo

  days after: thicker, lower

    Ac, As

  near surface:

    Ns, heavy contiuous rain,snow

    (less stable more cumuliform)

  after:

warm air over cold ground, stable     rapid clearing

 

 

Cold pools:[not on surface synoptic chart]

  polar air detaches at high ALT, into sub-tropical

   (opposite to inversion)

  vigorous convection; widespread rain, SH

  slowly sinks,warms

 

High pressure,anticyclone:

  isobars close around a high

  (direction, not speed)

  usually indicate:

     good weather(sinking air, prevent cloud)     little cloud, Cu, Sc during day

Ridge:

  region high elongate away from the sys.

  (common winter AUS)

  usually indicate:

     good weather, stable condition

  hazardous weather:

    tropical maritime（most humid）

    orographic,Great Dividing Range

coastal showers

 

 

Low pressure, depression:

  isobars close around low

  (very intense low: tropical cyclone)

  situated warm ocean(for tropical one)  humid

  coverges(depends on stability):

    Ns, Cb, heavy rain, showers

Trough:

   isobars elongate away from sys.

   dashed line:

     strong convergence: worst weather

   Monsoonal trough: dashed dotted line

 

 

Col:

  between two highs and two lows

  isobars convex, bending away

  opposite side: opposite direction, same value   typically:

     light, variable wind
