Fog:

  Radiation Fog:

     conduction with cold surface

     fog:<1000m;mist:>1000m

     conditions:

        clear skies night

        light winds

        humid air

     clearing:increase mixing,or temp up

        wind strength

        solar heating

           thickens before dissipates

  Advection Fog:

     warm humid air across cold surface

     any time, persist longer, forecast difficult      Atlantic(Titanic), rare in AUS

     S. high, along W. AUS, Tasman sea

Slant VIS:

   Viewed from an angle, not visible on final

Operational Significance:

   VIS <1000m in fog(IFR)

   1\ fog tend to flow in valley

   2\ lifted off ground, low St, high risk

   3\ extra fuel if possible fog(OPRs)

 

 

 

 

 

Mountain waves(Standing waves):

   lee side undulating flow

   Conditions:

      Wind direction: Right angle

      Wind speed: >25kt at mountain top(Winter westerly jet stream)

      Atmospheric Stability: unstable<stable<less stable(winter common)   effect:

      updraft, downdraft, severe turbulence; closed rotation: rotor

   danger:

      exceed max climb performance, descend, turn, impact

   Action:

      generous margin, 1000ft or so

      VFR, cloud base above:

         approach at oblique angle, less turn to escape if downdraft

   Clouds formation:

       humid, reach dew point:

          crest: lenticular clouds; rotor:sometimes roll clouds

   glider: parallel to ranges, upwind of lenticular cloud

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Airframe ice:

   supercooled water droplets

     Freeze:latent heat must be relased

     smaller: less efficient heat exchange, remain liquid lower temp

     disturbance, shock: lost shape, lost latent heat, freeze

   Rime ice:

     freeze on impact, no time to splash;

     white, brittle, air trapped in grains

     leading edge, mushroom shape, reduces L, increase D; Pitot tube      IFR, high level, supercooled droplets

 

Rime ice:

  on rotor blade;

  top, bottom free;  run-up

 

Clear ice:

   Larger supercooled water droplets; warmer sub-zero temp

    more latent heat to lose, longer time to freeze

   much heavier, stronger, remove difficult

  Conditions:

    subzero temperature: skin of AC

supercooled droplets: must liquid, not ice

    droplet size: small(DZ):rime; large: clear

 

 

Determine ice type:

  supercooled droplet size(almost):

          small(even 0,-15)rime

          large: clear

  temperature:

          0, -15:clear

          -10,-30:rime

          overlap: if small, large present: both

          below -30: likely frozen

Catch efficiency:

    thin cross-sectional area: higher catch efficiency;

    seperate closer to leading edge

First notice icing on thin structures: radio aerials, outboard wing

  water vapour deposits on surface

  (windscreen as well)

   momentary loss of VIS

TS

   conditions:

     atmospheric instablility

     high humidity

     trigger mechanism:

       initial lifting,latent heat released, ensure ascent

         Heat/convective TS

            solar heat, typical summer mid afternoon

         Frontal TS

            warm air rides over cold

         Cold stream TS

            latitudinal heating

         Nocturnal TS

ocean relatively warm; top loses heat, radiation

         Orographic TS: VC mountains, combined with convection,more frequent

         Convergence TS

             surface veered, low pressure trough, hundreds miles

 

 

Updraft

warm moist air sucked into leading edge,

  rise, overshoot dome, top: spread horizontally,anvil

 forward flank downdraft:air cools, sink, friction of rain, hail;

   cool, moist air,before cell,first effect of approaching storm!

rear flank downdraft:middle upper level dry air sucked into sides; sinks, warmer drier air

 

TS development

  developing

    updraft

  mature

    updraft, downdraft(falling droplets friction)

    horizontal wind shear,extreme turbulence,most serious hazard!    lightning

    squalls, heavy rain, hail

  dissipating

    downdrafts weaken

TS hazards

 Severe turbulence: near TS(especially mature),buffeting

 airframe ice:

strong updraft->large supercooled

    moderate to severe clear, rime ice

    especially middle above freezing

 Lightning: part of pathway, no immediate danger to occupants

       damage electrical equipment, fire danger

 Hail: clear air: beneath, beside downwind of cell.

       most frequent encounter: 10,000 - 20,000ft

 

 

 

 

 

 

Low level Hazards:

  Squalls, Gusts: control difficulty for low IAS, takeoff, landing

  Wind shear: loss of lift and IAS

  Micronursts:

(apart from main downdraft) localized, short lived blasts of cold air(indicated by virga)

  Tornadoes: unstable atmosphere,always with vigerous TS

          first:funnel-shaped cloud,

          touch ground: extreme low pressure(50hPa lower), air inside building explode. 500km/h wind 50km/h track

Dust storms: unstable but too dry for clouds

          Convection occurs,dust lifted,very wide area, serious reduction in VIS

Dust devil(willy willy) : localised low, mini-tornad

          lifts dust, short-lived

          over hot desert

Waterspouts: over sea: tornadoe from Cb, suck water through funnel cloud; dust devil oversea
